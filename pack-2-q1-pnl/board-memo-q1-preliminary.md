HAVEN & HEARTH HOME GOODS
Board Memo — Q1 2026 Preliminary Read

TO: Board of Directors
FROM: Jess Morales, Director of Growth Marketing
DATE: April 8, 2026
STATUS: Preliminary. Final numbers reconciled with Finance by April 22.

HEADLINE

Q1 came in at $2.71M total revenue, on a marketing spend of $890K. That puts us roughly 15% over our approved Q1 marketing budget of $774K. Revenue was up 18% year-over-year, driven almost entirely by online DTC and the subscription box.

THE OVERAGE

Nearly the entire variance sits in paid media. We planned $450K for the quarter and spent $562K — $112K over. January and February were on plan. March blew the budget: $252K against a $150K monthly plan.

Two things contributed:

1. Spring launch push. We leaned into Meta, Google, and TikTok harder than forecast to capitalize on the Home Ritual subscription momentum. ROAS held at 3.1x, so the incremental spend returned revenue — but we didn't raise a budget amendment before doing it.

2. Brightside Media. A new vendor I'm not familiar with ran $45K through our Paid Media line on 3/18. No PO, no campaign brief attached, no post-campaign report in our Drive. Finance flagged it in their end-of-month close. I'd like a written explanation from whoever engaged them before we issue any further payments to that vendor.

TOOLS OVERAGE

Tools came in at $31K against a $15K plan. $16K of that is the annual Anthropic Enterprise contract we pre-paid to unlock Claude Cowork for the team. Genuine one-time. Probably belongs in capex, not opex, for the reforecast.

THE BRIGHT SPOT

Creative production came in $20K under plan. Rachel Chen's in-house photography work has quietly been replacing what used to be $18K-per-shoot agency contracts. We should formalize her role in Q2.

Subscription box (Home Ritual) is up 80% year-over-year. It's still a small line ($135K for Q1), but the trajectory makes it our fastest-growing channel. I'd like Board alignment on whether to shift $75-100K from retail acquisition in Q2 into subscription acquisition.

THE QUESTION MARK

Amazon Marketplace was down 35% month-over-month in March. I've asked our Amazon team for a channel audit. First read is a combination of Buy Box losses on 3 SKUs and a content update that tanked organic ranking. I'll have a more complete read by the April 22 board packet.

ASK

1. Ratify the $112K paid media overage given the 3.1x ROAS.
2. Approve investigation and written response on Brightside Media.
3. Approve Q2 reallocation: $75K from retail acquisition to subscription acquisition, contingent on Amazon audit results.

I'll be in Tuesday's budget working session to walk through the full detail.

— Jess
