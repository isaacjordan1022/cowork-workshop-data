RestWell Closes $60 Million Series B to Expand Subscription Bedding Category
Round led by Felicis with participation from Forerunner and existing investors Greycroft and Lerer Hippeau

BROOKLYN, NY — RestWell, the subscription-first hotel-grade bedding brand, today announced the close of a $60 million Series B financing round. The round was led by Felicis Ventures, with participation from Forerunner Ventures and existing investors Greycroft and Lerer Hippeau. The investment will fuel a planned expansion into retail, deeper supply-chain integration, and continued investment in RestWell's proprietary fulfillment-on-cadence platform.

Founded in 2022 by Devon Price and Aisha Ramirez, RestWell pioneered a subscription model for bedding that replaces the one-and-done purchase cycle with a hotel-style refresh program. Customers choose their product mix, color, and refresh cadence — every 90, 180, or 365 days — and RestWell delivers a coordinated set before the previous one is ready to retire. The company reports more than 300,000 active subscribers and a net revenue retention above 140%.

"We started RestWell because bedding was the last category where the consumer experience felt stuck in 1998," said Devon Price, co-founder and CEO. "You bought sheets, and then you dealt with them for five years. Subscription changed music, film, groceries, coffee. It was time to change bedding."

"RestWell has built the subscription unit economics most home-goods brands have been trying to reach for a decade," said Aydin Senkut, founder of Felicis. "The combination of product quality, data-driven fulfillment, and genuinely useful refresh cadence is a category-defining platform."

The Series B will primarily fund three initiatives:

1. Retail expansion. RestWell plans to open flagship stores in New York, Los Angeles, Austin, and Chicago beginning in late 2026. The stores will serve as fitting rooms for first-time subscribers.

2. Supply-chain vertical integration. A portion of the raise will fund partial ownership of a previously-contracted Portugal cotton mill, with the goal of reducing per-unit cost and defending margin against rising CPMs in paid media.

3. Technology and personalization. RestWell will expand its personalization platform to include bath, tabletop, and sleep-adjacent categories.

RestWell grew revenue 130% year-over-year in 2025 to an annualized run rate in excess of $180 million. The company is privately held and does not disclose specific financials.
