THE STATE OF DTC HOME GOODS · 2025 YEAR-IN-REVIEW
February 2026

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

2025 was the year direct-to-consumer home goods brands re-learned the economics of media. Paid-social CAC rose 22% across the category, compressing unit economics for brands that hadn't yet built meaningful owned audiences. The brands that thrived either (a) entered the year with strong email/SMS lists and 30%+ repeat rate, or (b) aggressively diversified into creator-led organic content.

Revenue concentration shifted toward top-of-category brands. The largest ten DTC home brands captured an estimated 58% of category net new growth. Brands in the long tail faced the sharpest CAC pressure.

MEDIA ECONOMICS

Blended CAC across our DTC home goods panel of 40 brands:

- 2023: $42
- 2024: $51 (+21%)
- 2025: $62 (+22%)

The driver is almost entirely paid social. Meta ads CPMs increased 28% in 2025. TikTok CPMs rose 19%. Pinterest and Google Search remained comparatively stable.

Brands that moved earliest on creator-led content strategies outperformed. In our sample, brands that shifted more than 25% of their paid-social budget to creator partnerships saw CAC decline 14% year-over-year. Brands that stayed on brand-owned creative exclusively saw CAC increase 31%.

SUBSCRIPTION & RECURRING REVENUE

Subscription boxes, refills, and replenishment SKUs are the clearest growth story of 2025. Home Ritual (Haven & Hearth), Craftsman Candles (subscription candle), and Nest & Nook Linen Refresh (quarterly sheet rotation) all posted 60%+ year-over-year growth.

Subscription programs offer three compounding advantages:
1. Lower CAC over customer lifetime through predictable re-engagement
2. Better inventory planning
3. Sharper data signal for creative testing

We expect subscription programs to be table stakes for any DTC home brand above $10M in revenue by 2027.

RETAIL & WHOLESALE

Parachute, Boll & Branch, and Tuft & Needle continued to expand owned retail. We believe the unit-level economics of owned flagships now exceed wholesale margin for most brands in our panel — a reversal of the 2022 consensus view that retail was a brand-investment loss leader.

Wholesale margin continues to compress. Anthropologie and West Elm both repriced vendor terms in 2025, with the average brand in our panel ceding 3-4 points of gross margin on wholesale orders.

WHAT TO WATCH IN 2026

1. Creator-led paid media mix — expect this to keep rising as a share of total paid spend
2. Subscription attach rates — brands without a recurring SKU offer will face compounding CAC pressure
3. Retail vs. wholesale — expect more brands to exit wholesale entirely in favor of owned retail
4. AI-driven creative velocity — brands using generative tools are producing 3-4x more creative assets per dollar
