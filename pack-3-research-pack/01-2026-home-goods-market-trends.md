U.S. HOME GOODS — 2026 MARKET TRENDS
Quarterly Perspective · March 2026

AUTHORS: Lena Park, Principal Analyst · Marcus Tate, Associate Analyst

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The U.S. home goods category is reshaping in ways that favor vertically-integrated direct-to-consumer brands over traditional retailers. We see three forces at work through 2026 and into 2027.

First, subscription models are capturing a disproportionate share of category growth. Recurring-revenue home categories — coffee, cleaning supplies, linens, candles — are expanding 40-60% year-over-year while comparable transactional categories grow 8-12%.

Second, brick-and-mortar retail is bifurcating. Experience-forward flagship stores from brands like Parachute, Boll & Branch, and Nestling & Co. are outperforming their own e-commerce. Meanwhile, department store home goods concessions are flat-to-declining.

Third, wholesale concentration is a growing risk. Brands that depend on Anthropologie, West Elm, or CB2 for more than 25% of revenue are seeing margin compression as the big-box channel renegotiates terms.

CATEGORY GROWTH

Total U.S. home goods retail spend is projected at $421 billion in 2026, up 4.2% year-over-year. This is broadly in line with overall discretionary consumer spending and well below the 11% category growth observed in 2022.

But the growth is not evenly distributed:

- Subscription & repeat-purchase home (linens, cleaning, consumables): +42% YoY
- DTC premium bedding & bath: +14% YoY
- DTC kitchen & tabletop: +9% YoY
- Mid-market wholesale (Anthropologie, West Elm, CB2 feeders): +3% YoY
- Discount mass (TJX, Ross, Marshalls home): +6% YoY
- Department store home concessions: -4% YoY

The implication: brands positioned in the recurring-revenue and premium-DTC segments are capturing essentially all of the category's net dollar growth.

COMPETITIVE DYNAMICS

Three brands to watch through 2026:

RestWell (Series B, March 2026): Our channel checks indicate RestWell's subscription-box model is tracking to ~$180M in ARR by year-end, up from ~$95M in 2025. Their Customer Acquisition Cost has dropped 30% since integrating creator partnerships, which we attribute to genuine product-market fit rather than discounting. Their recent $60M Series B will likely fund retail expansion in late 2026.

Parachute Home: Growth has slowed to ~8% YoY, down from 22% in 2024. We believe the brand is approaching ceiling in its premium bedding niche and is evaluating adjacent categories. Watch for tabletop or bath expansion in H2.

Boll & Branch: The Tempur-Sealy acquisition completed Q3 2025 appears to have slowed product velocity. Our retail audits show 23% fewer new SKU introductions versus the prior twelve months. This creates a window for competitors to capture mindshare in organic-cotton bedding.

IMPLICATIONS FOR SMALLER BRANDS

For brands in the $8M-$40M annual revenue range — where most of our regional-DTC coverage sits — we see three 2026 priorities:

1. Evaluate a subscription adjacency. The recurring-revenue growth premium is structural, not cyclical.

2. Reduce wholesale concentration below 25% of revenue where possible. Leverage is moving to the retailer.

3. Double down on in-house creative production. The brands winning on paid efficiency are the ones who have brought photography, copy, and performance motion in-house.
